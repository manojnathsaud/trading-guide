Liquidity Pools & Stop Clusters

Liquidity pools are areas where many stop-loss orders accumulate.

These areas attract price because they provide the volume institutions need.

Two Types of Liquidity

1. Buy-Side Liquidity (BSL)

This exists above price.

Where it forms:

  • Above previous highs
  • Above resistance
  • Above equal highs

Why?

Short sellers place their stop losses above highs.

When price reaches these levels:

  • Stops trigger
  • Liquidity becomes available
  • Institutions can sell into it.

2. Sell-Side Liquidity (SSL)

This exists below price.

Where it forms:

  • Below previous lows
  • Below support
  • Below equal lows

Why?

Long traders place stop losses below lows.

When price hits these levels:

  • Stops trigger
  • Institutions can buy into the selling pressure.

Where Liquidity Usually Builds

Common liquidity zones:

✔ Equal highs
✔ Equal lows
✔ Previous day high/low
✔ Support & resistance
✔ Trendline levels
✔ Round numbers

The more obvious the level → the bigger the liquidity pool.

Liquidity Sweep (Stop Hunt)

Institutions often push price slightly beyond liquidity.

Example:

Price moves above equal highs →
Stops trigger →
Market reverses.

This is called:

Liquidity Sweep
or
Stop Hunt

Typical Smart Money Trade

A common institutional move:

This is why markets often break levels and reverse.

Example Trade

Equal highs form on BTC.

Retail traders:

Market moves slightly above highs.

Stops trigger → liquidity created.

Then price reverses downward.

This traps breakout traders.

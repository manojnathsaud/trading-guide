📖 1. What is Trading?

Trading is the act of buying and selling financial instruments (stocks, currencies, crypto, commodities) to make profit from price movements.

  • Investing = long-term holding (months/years)
  • Trading = short-term buying/selling (minutes to weeks)

Types of Traders:

  • Scalper — holds for seconds to minutes
  • Day Trader — opens and closes within the same day
  • Swing Trader — holds for days to weeks
  • Position Trader — holds for weeks to months
Choose your style based on your time availability, risk tolerance, and personality.

🌍 2. Types of Markets

  • Stock Market — NSE, BSE (India), NYSE (US)
  • Forex Market — Currency pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/JPY)
  • Crypto Market — BTC, ETH, altcoins
  • Commodity Market — Gold, Silver, Crude Oil
  • Derivatives — Futures & Options
Start with one market and master it before moving to others.

📊 3. Chart Types

Line Chart

Connects closing prices. Simple but lacks detail.

Bar Chart (OHLC)

Shows Open, High, Low, Close for each period.

Candlestick Chart

Most popular. Shows OHLC with colored bodies.

Use candlestick charts — they give the most information at a glance.

🕯️ 4. Candlestick Basics

Each candlestick shows 4 data points:

  • Open — where price started
  • High — highest price reached
  • Low — lowest price reached
  • Close — where price ended
Bullish Candle (Green/White)

Close > Open → buyers won

Bearish Candle (Red/Black)

Close < Open → sellers won

Anatomy:

  • Body — distance between open and close
  • Upper Wick/Shadow — high minus body top
  • Lower Wick/Shadow — body bottom minus low
Long body = strong momentum. Long wick = rejection at that level.

🔥 5. Candlestick Patterns

Single Candle Patterns:

  • Doji — indecision, open ≈ close
  • Hammer — bullish reversal at bottom (long lower wick)
  • Shooting Star — bearish reversal at top (long upper wick)
  • Marubozu — full body, no wicks (strong momentum)
  • Spinning Top — small body, long wicks (indecision)

Double Candle Patterns:

  • Bullish Engulfing — green candle fully engulfs previous red candle
  • Bearish Engulfing — red candle fully engulfs previous green candle
  • Tweezer Top/Bottom — two candles with equal highs/lows

Triple Candle Patterns:

  • Morning Star — bullish reversal (bearish → doji → bullish)
  • Evening Star — bearish reversal (bullish → doji → bearish)
  • Three White Soldiers — 3 strong bullish candles in a row
  • Three Black Crows — 3 strong bearish candles in a row
⚠ Context matters! A hammer at support is strong. A hammer in the middle of nowhere is meaningless.

📏 6. Support & Resistance

Support = price level where buying pressure prevents further decline.

Resistance = price level where selling pressure prevents further rise.

How to Identify:

  • Previous highs and lows
  • Round numbers (₹100, ₹500, ₹1000)
  • Areas where price bounced multiple times
  • Moving averages acting as dynamic S/R

Key Rules:

  • The more times a level is tested → stronger it becomes
  • When support breaks → it becomes resistance (and vice versa)
  • Use zones, not exact lines — markets are not precise
Role Reversal is one of the most powerful concepts in trading.

📈 7. Trend Analysis

Uptrend
  • Higher Highs (HH)
  • Higher Lows (HL)
Downtrend
  • Lower Highs (LH)
  • Lower Lows (LL)
Sideways / Range
  • Equal Highs and Equal Lows
  • No clear direction

Trendlines:

  • Connect 2+ swing lows for uptrend trendline
  • Connect 2+ swing highs for downtrend trendline
  • More touches = stronger trendline
"The trend is your friend" — always trade in the direction of the trend.

📐 8. Chart Patterns

Reversal Patterns:

  • Head & Shoulders — bearish reversal
  • Inverse Head & Shoulders — bullish reversal
  • Double Top — bearish (M pattern)
  • Double Bottom — bullish (W pattern)
  • Triple Top / Triple Bottom

Continuation Patterns:

  • Flag / Pennant — brief pause then trend continues
  • Ascending Triangle — bullish breakout likely
  • Descending Triangle — bearish breakout likely
  • Symmetrical Triangle — breakout either way
  • Cup & Handle — bullish continuation
  • Wedge — rising wedge (bearish), falling wedge (bullish)
⚠ Wait for breakout confirmation before entering. Don't anticipate pattern completion.

🔧 9. Indicators & Oscillators

Moving Averages:

  • SMA (Simple Moving Average) — average of last N candles
  • EMA (Exponential Moving Average) — gives more weight to recent prices
  • Common periods: 9, 20, 50, 100, 200
  • Golden Cross — 50 EMA crosses above 200 EMA (bullish)
  • Death Cross — 50 EMA crosses below 200 EMA (bearish)

RSI (Relative Strength Index):

  • Measures momentum (0–100)
  • Above 70 = overbought (potential reversal down)
  • Below 30 = oversold (potential reversal up)
  • Look for divergence between price and RSI

MACD:

  • MACD Line = 12 EMA - 26 EMA
  • Signal Line = 9 EMA of MACD Line
  • Crossovers indicate momentum shifts

Bollinger Bands:

  • Middle band = 20 SMA
  • Upper/Lower bands = 2 standard deviations
  • Squeeze = low volatility, expansion coming
Don't use too many indicators. Pick 1-2 and master them. Indicators CONFIRM — they don't predict.

📊 10. Volume Analysis

Volume shows how many shares/contracts were traded.

Good Signs:

  • Breakout with high volume → genuine move
  • Volume increasing in trend direction → trend strength
  • Volume spike at key levels → institutional activity

Warning Signs:

  • Breakout on low volume → likely fake breakout
  • Price up + volume decreasing → weakening trend
  • Sudden volume spike + reversal → trap
Volume precedes price. Always check volume before trusting a move.

🛡️ 11. Risk Management Basics

  • Never risk more than 1-2% of capital per trade
  • Always use a stop loss
  • Minimum risk-reward ratio: 1:2
  • Position size = (Account Risk) / (Stop Loss Distance)
Example:
Account: ₹1,00,000
Risk per trade (1%): ₹1,000
Stop loss: ₹10 away
Position size: 1000 / 10 = 100 shares
Capital preservation is priority #1. You can't trade if you blow your account.

📝 12. Order Types

  • Market Order — buy/sell immediately at current price
  • Limit Order — buy/sell at a specific price or better
  • Stop Loss Order — automatically exits at your defined loss level
  • Stop Limit Order — stop loss that converts to limit order
  • Trailing Stop — stop loss that moves with price
Use limit orders for entries and always have a stop loss in place.

📋 13. Building a Trading Plan

Every successful trader has a written plan. Include:

  • Market — what do you trade?
  • Timeframe — what chart timeframe?
  • Strategy — what setups do you take?
  • Entry Rules — specific conditions to enter
  • Exit Rules — stop loss and target levels
  • Risk Rules — max risk per trade, per day
  • Journal — record every trade
⭐ "Fail to plan = Plan to fail"
A trading plan removes emotion and keeps you disciplined.

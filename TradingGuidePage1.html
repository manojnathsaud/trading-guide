1. Timeframe Strategy

Primary timeframe:

  • 15-minute candles for trade setups (entries and exits).

Supporting timeframes:

  • 1m / 5m → to understand immediate price action.
  • 15m → main confirmation and trading decisions.

2. Price Action (Foundation)

Price action is the most important concept in trading.

Focus on how price moves, not just candle colors.

Market Structure

Understand the basic trend structure:

Uptrend

  • Higher Highs (HH)
  • Higher Lows (HL)

Downtrend

  • Lower Highs (LH)
  • Lower Lows (LL)

Key Levels

Always mark:

Support

  • Level where buyers step in.

Resistance

  • Level where sellers step in.

Big moves usually happen after price consolidates near these levels.

Patience is important.

3. Chart Patterns & Momentum

Recognize common patterns that show momentum or reversal.

Reversal Patterns

  • Double Top
  • Double Bottom
  • M Pattern
  • W Pattern

Continuation Patterns

  • Flags
  • Triangles
  • Channels

Simple price swings often signal:

  • trend continuation
  • trend reversal

4. Candlestick Patterns

Use candlestick patterns with context, not alone.

Bullish Patterns

  • Bullish Engulfing
  • Hammer
  • Morning Star

Bearish Patterns

  • Shooting Star
  • Evening Star
  • Dark Cloud Cover

Important rule:
Context > Pattern

A candle pattern only matters near support/resistance or trend levels.

5. Volume Understanding

Volume shows participation of big players.

Important insights:

  • Large candles do NOT always mean large volume.
  • Small candles can have large volumes during high liquidity.
  • Sudden large candles or gaps often indicate institutional activity.

Always watch price + volume together.

6. Breakouts & Traps

Breakouts are powerful but often used as traps.

Common situations:

True Breakout

  • Break + retest
  • Strong volume
  • Trend continuation

Fake Breakout

  • Quick breakout then immediate reversal
  • Traps late buyers or sellers

Never enter a breakout without context.

7. Risk Management (MOST IMPORTANT)

Even the best strategy fails without risk management.

Position Sizing

Never risk more than:

1–2% of capital per trade

Risk/Reward

Minimum: 1:2 or 1:3

Example:

Risk = ₹100
Target = ₹200 or ₹300

Stop Loss

Always know the stop loss BEFORE entry.

Capital Protection Rules

  • Never revenge trade
  • Never increase size after losses
  • Preserve capital first

8. Trade Management

Once in a trade:

  1. Take partial profit at 1:3 RR
  2. Move stop loss to break-even
  3. Trail stop to capture bigger trends

9. Trading Discipline Rules

Key psychological rules:

  • Wait for clear setups
  • Do not force trades
  • Follow rules consistently
  • Keep charts clean and simple
  • Avoid emotional decisions

Important rule:

Don't trade just because the market is open.

Trade only when your system gives a signal.

10. Daily Trading Rules

Practical trading rules:

  • Maximum 3 stop losses per day
  • After 3 losses → stop trading
  • Re-entry allowed but controlled
  • Avoid revenge trading

11. Trend Following Philosophy

The goal is not frequent wins.

The goal is:

Catch 1–2 big trends per week

Small losses are normal.

One large trend can compensate for many small losses.

12. Position Size Adjustment

Adjust quantity based on market direction:

Trade with trend → larger size

Trade against trend → smaller size

13. Trading Process (The Complete System)

A proper trading system includes:

  1. Timeframe selection
  2. Market trend analysis
  3. Chart selection
  4. Position sizing
  5. Entry signal
  6. Stop-loss placement
  7. Trailing stop
  8. Profit target
  9. Trade management
  10. Post-trade analysis

14. Mindset & Psychology

Trading is mostly psychology.

Markets are driven by:

  • Fear
  • Greed

Key truths:

  • You will rarely catch exact tops or bottoms
  • Losses are part of the process
  • Focus only on the current trade

Your first major goal:

Become a break-even trader

Most traders never reach this stage.

15. Bear Market Survival Tips

  1. Plan trades in advance
  2. Be patient with entries
  3. Reduce position size
  4. Adjust for higher volatility
  5. Always have an exit strategy
  6. Zoom out to see bigger trends
  7. Cash is also a position
  8. Avoid panic and emotional decisions
  9. Avoid FOMO
  10. Focus on risk control

16. Tips for New Traders

  • Reduce your watchlist
  • Trade smaller size
  • Follow the path of least resistance (trend)
  • Ignore opinions and news noise
  • Exit losing trades quickly
  • Don't hold losing trades hoping for recovery

17. Trader Development Process

Important components beyond setups:

  1. Position sizing
  2. Trade management
  3. Portfolio management
  4. Backtesting
  5. Trading journal
  6. Post-trade review
  7. Mindset development

19. Final Trading Principles

Successful traders follow these rules:

  1. Enter only when there is an edge
  2. Define stop loss before entry
  3. Maintain favorable risk/reward
  4. Manage profits actively
  5. Exit when the trend reverses
  6. Never add to losing trades
  7. Trade only within a complete system

💡 The most important lesson:

Trading success comes from:

Risk Management + Discipline + Consistency

Not from finding a magical setup.

Part 1 — Trading Guide eBook
01
Timeframe Strategy

Primary Timeframe

  • 15-minute candles — for trade setups (entries and exits).

Supporting Timeframes

  • 1m / 5m → to understand immediate price action.
  • 15m → main confirmation and trading decisions.
02
Price Action (Foundation)

Price action is the most important concept in trading. Focus on how price moves, not just candle colors.

Market Structure

Understand the basic trend structure:

📈 Uptrend

  • Higher Highs (HH)
  • Higher Lows (HL)

📉 Downtrend

  • Lower Highs (LH)
  • Lower Lows (LL)

Key Levels

Always mark:

Support

Level where buyers step in.

Resistance

Level where sellers step in.

Big moves usually happen after price consolidates near these levels. Patience is important.

03
Chart Patterns & Momentum

Recognize common patterns that show momentum or reversal.

Reversal Patterns

  • Double Top
  • Double Bottom
  • M Pattern
  • W Pattern

Continuation Patterns

  • Flags
  • Triangles
  • Channels

Simple price swings often signal:

  • Trend continuation
  • Trend reversal
04
Candlestick Patterns

Use candlestick patterns with context, not alone.

Bullish Patterns

  • Bullish Engulfing
  • Hammer
  • Morning Star

Bearish Patterns

  • Shooting Star
  • Evening Star
  • Dark Cloud Cover

Important rule: Context > Pattern
A candle pattern only matters near support/resistance or trend levels.

05
Volume Understanding

Volume shows participation of big players.

Important Insights

  • Large candles do NOT always mean large volume.
  • Small candles can have large volumes during high liquidity.
  • Sudden large candles or gaps often indicate institutional activity.

Always watch price + volume together.

06
Breakouts & Traps

Breakouts are powerful but often used as traps.

✅ True Breakout

  • Break + retest
  • Strong volume
  • Trend continuation

⚠️ Fake Breakout

  • Quick breakout then immediate reversal
  • Traps late buyers or sellers

Never enter a breakout without context.

07
Risk Management MOST IMPORTANT

Even the best strategy fails without risk management.

Position Sizing

Never risk more than:

1–2% of capital per trade

Risk / Reward

Minimum: 1:2 or 1:3

Example:

Risk₹100
Target (1:2)₹200
Target (1:3)₹300

Stop Loss

Always know the stop loss BEFORE entry.

Capital Protection Rules

  • Never revenge trade
  • Never increase size after losses
  • Preserve capital first
08
Trade Management

Once in a trade:

  1. Take partial profit at 1:3 RR
  2. Move stop loss to break-even
  3. Trail stop to capture bigger trends
09
Trading Discipline Rules

Key psychological rules:

  • Wait for clear setups
  • Do not force trades
  • Follow rules consistently
  • Keep charts clean and simple
  • Avoid emotional decisions

Don't trade just because the market is open.
Trade only when your system gives a signal.

10
Daily Trading Rules

Practical trading rules:

  • Maximum 3 stop losses per day
  • After 3 losses → stop trading
  • Re-entry allowed but controlled
  • Avoid revenge trading
11
Trend Following Philosophy

The goal is not frequent wins.

Catch 1–2 big trends per week

  • Small losses are normal.
  • One large trend can compensate for many small losses.
12

Against Trend

→ Smaller size

Final Trading Principles

Successful traders follow these rules:

  1. Enter only when there is an edge
  2. Define stop loss before entry
  3. Maintain favorable risk/reward
  4. Manage profits actively
  5. Exit when the trend reverses
  6. Never add to losing trades

Trading success comes from:
Risk Management + Discipline + Consistency

Part 3 — Institutional Price Action Strategy
IPA
3-Step Institutional Price Action Strategy

This strategy works best on:

  • BTC / Crypto
  • Nifty / BankNifty
  • Forex
  • High-volume stocks

Best timeframe: 15m for structure + 5m for entry

S1
Step 1 — Liquidity Grab

Markets move where liquidity exists.

Liquidity = stop losses of retail traders

Institutions push price to these areas to trigger stops.

Common Liquidity Zones

  • Previous High
  • Previous Low
  • Equal Highs
  • Equal Lows
  • Support / Resistance

Retail Traders

  • Buy breakout above resistance
  • Place stop below support

Institutions

  • Push price above resistance
  • Trigger breakout buyers
  • Then reverse the market

This is called a Stop Hunt.
Your job: ✓ Identify where stops are located.

S2
Step 2 — Market Structure Shift (MSS)

After liquidity is taken, watch for structure break.

Example — Downtrend structure:

  • Lower High → Lower Low
  • Then suddenly price breaks the last lower high

That means: Market Structure Shift (MSS)
Now institutions are likely changing direction.

Signs of MSS

  • Strong impulse move
  • Break of previous structure
  • Large momentum candles

Trend reversal may start.

S3
Step 3 — Institutional Entry (Pullback)

Institutions rarely enter at the breakout. They enter on pullbacks.

Process

  1. Liquidity sweep
  2. Structure break
  3. Pullback to entry zone

Entry Zones

  • Order block
  • Support / resistance flip
  • Fair value gap
  • Breakout retest

This is where risk is lowest.

EX
Example Trade Flow
  1. Price sweeps previous high
  2. Liquidity taken
  3. Strong bearish candle breaks structure
  4. Price pulls back to resistance
  5. Enter short trade

Stop Loss

Above pullback high

Target

Next support level

💡
The Institutional Trade Formula
Liquidity Grab
↓
Market Structure Shift
↓
Pullback Entry
↓
Trend Move

This is how smart money trades.

WHY
Why This Strategy Works

❌ Retail Traders

  • Buy breakouts
  • Enter too late
  • Place obvious stop losses

✅ Institutions

  • Trigger stops
  • Create liquidity
  • Enter after traps

So instead of trading with retail, you trade with institutions.

✅
Professional Entry Checklist

Before entering trade confirm:

  • Liquidity taken
  • Structure break
  • Pullback entry
  • Risk-reward ≥ 1:2
  • Trade aligned with higher timeframe

If any condition is missing → skip trade.

🔑
The Real Secret

The market moves in this cycle:

Accumulation
↓
Manipulation (= Liquidity Grab)
↓
Distribution

Most traders lose money during manipulation.
Professionals trade after manipulation.

Part 4 — The 4-Candle Reversal Setup
4C
The 4-Candle Reversal Setup

The setup uses 4 consecutive candles to detect when momentum is shifting.

It works because markets often show momentum exhaustion before reversing.

STR
Structure of the Pattern

The pattern consists of four candles.

Candle 1 – Trend Candle
  • Strong candle in the current trend direction.
  • Shows the trend still has momentum.

Example: In a downtrend → strong bearish candle.

Candle 2 – Weak Continuation
  • Price continues slightly in the same direction.
  • Momentum starts slowing.

This signals trend exhaustion may begin.

Candle 3 – Indecision Candle
  • Small body / doji / rejection wick.
  • Buyers and sellers are fighting.

This is the warning sign that momentum is fading.

Candle 4 – Reversal Candle
  • Strong candle in the opposite direction.
  • Closes beyond Candle 3.

This confirms the reversal.

↑
Bullish 4-Candle Setup

Occurs after a downtrend.

Structure

  1. Strong bearish candle
  2. Smaller bearish candle
  3. Indecision / doji
  4. Strong bullish candle

Entry

Buy above Candle 4 high

Stop Loss

Below Candle 3 or 4 low

Target: Next resistance level.

↓
Bearish 4-Candle Setup

Occurs after an uptrend.

Structure

  1. Strong bullish candle
  2. Smaller bullish candle
  3. Indecision candle
  4. Strong bearish candle

Entry

Sell below Candle 4 low

Stop Loss

Above Candle 3 high

Target: Next support level.

📍
Best Locations for This Setup

Works Best At

  • Support
  • Resistance
  • Trend exhaustion
  • Liquidity sweeps
  • After strong trend moves

Avoid Trading In

  • Sideways markets
  • Middle of a range
VOL
Volume Confirmation

High probability when:

  • Reversal candle has higher volume
  • Trend candle volume decreases before reversal
🔍
Why This Pattern Works

Markets reverse because:

  1. Trend becomes overextended
  2. Momentum slows
  3. Traders take profits
  4. Opposite side enters

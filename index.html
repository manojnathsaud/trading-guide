2. Price Action (Foundation)

Price action is the most important concept in trading.

Focus on how price moves, not just candle colors.

Market Structure

Understand the basic trend structure:

Uptrend

  • Higher Highs (HH)
  • Higher Lows (HL)

Downtrend

  • Lower Highs (LH)
  • Lower Lows (LL)

Key Levels

Always mark:

Support

  • Level where buyers step in.

Resistance

  • Level where sellers step in.

Big moves usually happen after price consolidates near these levels.

Patience is important.

Reversal Patterns

  • Double Bottom

  • W Pattern

Continuation Patterns

  • Flags
  • Triangles

Simple price swings often signal:

5. Volume Understanding

Volume shows participation of big players.

Important insights:

  • Large candles do NOT always mean large volume.
  • Small candles can have large volumes during high liquidity.
  • Sudden large candles or gaps often indicate institutional activity.

Always watch price + volume together.

6. Breakouts & Traps

Breakouts are powerful but often used as traps.

Common situations:

True Breakout

  • Break + retest
  • Strong volume
  • Trend continuation

Fake Breakout

  • Quick breakout then immediate reversal
  • Traps late buyers or sellers

Never enter a breakout without context.

7. Risk Management (MOST IMPORTANT)

Even the best strategy fails without risk management.

Position Sizing

Never risk more than:

1–2% of capital per trade

Risk/Reward

Minimum: 1:2 or 1:3

Example:

Risk = ₹100
Target = ₹200 or ₹300

Stop Loss

Always know the stop loss BEFORE entry.

Capital Protection Rules

  • Never revenge trade
  • Never increase size after losses
  • Preserve capital first

Your job:

After liquidity is taken, watch for structure break.

Example:

Downtrend structure:

Lower High
Lower Low

Then suddenly price breaks the last lower high.

That means:

Market Structure Shift (MSS)

Now institutions are likely changing direction.

Signs of MSS:

✔ Strong impulse move
✔ Break of previous structure
✔ Large momentum candles

This tells you:

Trend reversal may start.

Institutions rarely enter at the breakout.

Process:

Entry zones:

• Order block

• Fair value gap
• Breakout retest

This is where risk is lowest.

The 4-Candle Reversal Setup

The setup uses 4 consecutive candles to detect when momentum is shifting.

It works because markets often show momentum exhaustion before reversing.

Structure of the Pattern

The pattern consists of four candles.

Candle 1 – Trend Candle

  • Strong candle in the current trend direction.
  • Shows the trend still has momentum.

Example
In a downtrend → strong bearish candle.

Candle 2 – Weak Continuation

  • Price continues slightly in the same direction.
  • Momentum starts slowing.

This signals trend exhaustion may begin.

Candle 3 – Indecision Candle

  • Small body / doji / rejection wick.
  • Buyers and sellers are fighting.

This is the warning sign that momentum is fading.

Candle 4 – Reversal Candle

  • Strong candle in the opposite direction.
  • Closes beyond Candle 3.

This confirms the reversal.

Bullish 4-Candle Setup

Occurs after a downtrend.

Structure:

1️⃣ Strong bearish candle
2️⃣ Smaller bearish candle
3️⃣ Indecision / doji
4️⃣ Strong bullish candle

Entry:

Buy above Candle 4 high

Stop Loss:

Below Candle 3 or Candle 4 low

Target:

Next resistance level.

Bearish 4-Candle Setup

Occurs after an uptrend.

Structure:

1️⃣ Strong bullish candle
2️⃣ Smaller bullish candle
3️⃣ Indecision candle
4️⃣ Strong bearish candle

Entry:

Sell below Candle 4 low

Stop Loss:

Above Candle 3 high

Target:

Next support level.

Best Locations for This Setup

This setup works best when it appears at:

✔ Support
✔ Resistance
✔ Trend exhaustion
✔ Liquidity sweeps
✔ After strong trend moves

Avoid trading it:

❌ In sideways markets
❌ In the middle of a range

Volume Confirmation

High probability when:

✔ Reversal candle has higher volume
✔ Trend candle volume decreases before reversal

Why This Pattern Works

Markets reverse because:

  1. Trend becomes overextended
  2. Momentum slows
  3. Traders take profits
  4. Opposite side enters

The 4-candle pattern captures this transition phase.

The 7 Phases of a Market Cycle

Markets repeat a cycle driven by human psychology: fear → optimism → greed → panic.

Phase 1 — Accumulation

This is where smart money begins buying quietly.

Characteristics:

  • Market moving sideways
  • Low volatility
  • Low public interest
  • Institutions slowly accumulating positions

Retail traders usually ignore this phase.

Opportunity:

  • Early long positions.

Phase 2 — Early Breakout

Price breaks above the accumulation range.

Signs:

  • Resistance breakout
  • Increase in volume
  • Momentum beginning

Traders start noticing the move.

Opportunity:

  • Breakout trades.

Phase 3 — Markup (Trend Phase)

This is the strong trending phase.

Characteristics:

  • Higher highs
  • Higher lows
  • Strong momentum
  • Media attention begins

Most profits are made in this phase.

Opportunity:

  • Trend following
  • Pullback entries

Phase 4 — Late Trend / Euphoria

Retail traders rush in.

Signs:

  • Rapid price increases
  • Social media hype
  • News coverage everywhere
  • Parabolic moves

Risk becomes very high.

Smart money starts selling into strength.

Phase 5 — Distribution

Institutions begin offloading positions.

Characteristics:

  • Sideways range after big rally
  • Volatility increases
  • Fake breakouts
  • Large wicks

Retail traders keep buying while smart money sells.

Opportunity:

  • Prepare for short positions.

Phase 6 — Markdown (Bear Trend)

Price starts falling.

Signs:

  • Lower highs
  • Lower lows
  • Panic selling
  • Negative news

Retail traders panic and sell at losses.

Opportunity:

  • Short trades.

Phase 7 — Capitulation

Final panic.

Characteristics:

  • Massive sell-offs
  • Fear everywhere
  • Traders exiting the market

This is often the best buying opportunity.

After this phase, the cycle returns to accumulation.

Characteristics:

  • Institutional participation

This is where traders should ride the move.

Then strong sell-off →
Distribution downward trend.

Liquidity Pools & Stop Clusters

Liquidity pools are areas where many stop-loss orders accumulate.

These areas attract price because they provide the volume institutions need.

Two Types of Liquidity

1. Buy-Side Liquidity (BSL)

This exists above price.

Where it forms:

  • Above previous highs
  • Above resistance
  • Above equal highs

Why?

Short sellers place their stop losses above highs.

When price reaches these levels:

  • Stops trigger
  • Liquidity becomes available
  • Institutions can sell into it.

2. Sell-Side Liquidity (SSL)

This exists below price.

Where it forms:

  • Below previous lows
  • Below support
  • Below equal lows

Why?

Long traders place stop losses below lows.

When price hits these levels:

  • Stops trigger
  • Institutions can buy into the selling pressure.

Where Liquidity Usually Builds

Common liquidity zones:

✔ Equal highs
✔ Equal lows
✔ Previous day high/low
✔ Support & resistance
✔ Trendline levels
✔ Round numbers

The more obvious the level → the bigger the liquidity pool.

Liquidity Sweep (Stop Hunt)

Institutions often push price slightly beyond liquidity.
